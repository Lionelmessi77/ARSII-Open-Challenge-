# World Labs Tourism Prompts - Tunisia

## Sidi Bou Said
```
A picturesque blue and white village perched on cliffs overlooking the Mediterranean Sea. Narrow cobblestone streets winding between whitewashed buildings with bright blue doors and windows. Bougainvillea flowers cascading from walls. Panoramic sea views with small boats in the harbor below. Traditional Tunisian architecture with Andalusian influences. Cafe terraces with people enjoying mint tea. The iconic blue-domed minaret standing tall against the sky. Warm golden hour lighting casting long shadows across the scene.
```

## El Jem Colosseum
```
The magnificent Roman amphitheater of El Jem, one of the best-preserved Roman colosseums in the world. Massive stone arches rising three stories high, creating a sense of ancient grandeur. The vast arena floor with sunlight streaming through the arches. Intricate stonework showing Roman engineering mastery. The golden sand of Tunisia contrasting with weathered limestone. Visitors walking through ancient passages. Views from the upper tiers looking out over the modern town. A testament to Rome's African empire.
```

## Carthage Ruins
```
Ancient Carthage overlooking the Gulf of Tunis. Weathered Roman columns standing against deep blue Mediterranean waters. The Antonine Baths with their grand arches and sea views. Archaeological remains scattered across a hillside. Punic and Roman layers of history intermingled. Olive trees and Mediterranean vegetation among ruins. White marble stones warmed by Tunisian sun. The Tophet sanctuary with ancient stelae. Where ancient civilizations once ruled the Mediterranean.
```

## Sahara Desert - Douz
```
The golden Sahara Desert near Douz with endless rolling dunes. Rippled sand patterns created by desert winds. A caravan of camels walking across orange sand dunes at sunset. Traditional Bedouin tents dotting the landscape. The vast emptiness of the desert under brilliant blue sky. Palm trees of the oasis creating green contrasts. Starry night sky with the Milky Way visible. The silence and serenity of endless sand. Golden hour lighting creating dramatic shadows on dunes.
```

## Djerba Island
```
The white sandy beaches of Djerba island with turquoise Mediterranean waters. Traditional whitewashed buildings with flat roofs. The famous El Ghriba synagogue with its colorful tiles. Palm trees swaying along the shore. Fishing boats in the harbor. The legendary island where Odysseus was said to be stranded. Warm sunshine and gentle sea breeze. Beach resorts with thatched umbrellas. The blend of Tunisian, Jewish, and Mediterranean cultures.
```

## Kairouan - Holy City
```
The ancient holy city of Kairouan with its imposing Great Mosque of Okba. Massive stone walls and minarets rising above the medina. Narrow streets of the old medina with whitewashed houses. Traditional carpet workshops with artisans weaving. The ancient basins of the Aghlabid water reservoirs. Calligraphy and Islamic geometric patterns decorating buildings. The spiritual atmosphere of one of Islam's holiest cities. Sunlight filtering through mosque arches onto marble floors.
```

## Medina of Tunis
```
The bustling UNESCO World Heritage medina of Tunis. A labyrinth of narrow covered souks (markets) with goods spilling onto streets. Traditional Tunisian doors with intricate patterns. Spices mounded in colorful pyramids. Craftspeople working in small shops. The scent of mint tea and grilled lamb. The Zitouna Mosque's minaret rising above the rooftops. Blue and white architectural details. The energy of daily commerce in historic surroundings.
```

## Hammamet Beaches
```
The pristine beaches of Hammamet with sparkling Mediterranean waters. White sand stretching along the coast. The historic Hammamet Fort overlooking the sea. Luxury resorts with pools and gardens. Jasmine flowers scenting the air. Orange and pomegranate groves nearby. Traditional Tunisian pottery and ceramics. The perfect blend of relaxation and culture. Golden beaches meeting azure waters.
```

## Chefchaouen (Bonus - Northern Morocco)
```
The famous blue city of Chefchaouen with its powder-blue buildings cascading down a mountainside. Narrow cobblestone stairs winding between blue-washed walls. Colorful woven rugs displayed in markets. The Rif Mountains rising in the background. Cats lounging in sunny spots. Potted plants adding splashes of green. Doorways and archways painted in every shade of blue. A photographer's paradise with every corner offering a perfect shot.
```

## Matmata Troglodyte Homes
```
The unique underground troglodyte homes of Matmata. Craters in the earth leading to subterranean dwellings. White courtyards carved into the rock. Circular rooms creating natural coolness. Traditional Berber architecture adapted to desert life. The landscape appearing lunar-like. Star Wars filming locations nearby. Simple white interiors with colorful textiles. A millennia-old way of living that survives today.
```

## Ichkeul National Park
```
The serene wetlands of Ichkeul National Park. Mirroring lake waters reflecting the sky. Flocks of migrating flamingos, storks, and geese. Rolling hills surrounding the lake. Unique ecosystem where fresh and saltwater meet. Rare bird species in their natural habitat. Lush vegetation around the shores. Peaceful wilderness just hours from Tunis. A UNESCO protected biosphere reserve.
```
